# Evaluation Notes

- SWE-bench and related benchmarks are useful for measuring agent progress, but public benchmark exposure can make scores less trustworthy over time.
- Verified subsets reduce noisy labels but do not eliminate contamination, overfitting, or hidden-test gaming.
- Practical review policy: require repository-local evidence, not just a patch shape that resembles known benchmark fixes.
- Fresh tests, exact failure reproduction, and regression coverage are stronger acceptance signals than benchmark familiarity.
